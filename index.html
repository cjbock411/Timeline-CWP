• 1975 Clearwing Audio
    Under the name Clearwing Audio, Gregg Brunclik, President/CEO, and his business partner start providing sound engineering for local events and festivals in Milwaukee, Wisconsin.
  • 1976 Clearwing Audio
    With parts from local electronics and hardware stores, Gregg builds speakers in his dad’s basement in Milwaukee, Wisconsin.

    “We literally built the business from four by eight sheets of plywood; that’s where we started,” Gregg said.
  • 1979 Clearwing Audio
    Eddie Rabbitt, on tour with hit song “I Love a Rainy Night,” books a show at the Milwaukee Performing Arts Center and brings on Clearwing Audio for sound engineering. After the show, Eddie Rabbitt asks Gregg if Clearwing could be in Norman, Oklahoma, three days later.
  • 1981 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio goes on tour with Blackfoot and Def Leppard on Def Leppard’s High ‘n’ Dry tour.
  • 1983 Clearwing Audio
    My first employer. All the stuff I've learned and projects I've been working on.
  • 1985 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio is officially launched as an incorporated company.
  • 1988 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio provides audio and lighting for Harley-Davidson’s 85th Anniversary Celebration in Milwaukee, and becomes a part of the company’s anniversary every five years.
  • 1989 Clearwing Audio
    The World Wrestling Federation, today known as WWE, comes to Milwaukee for a three-day TV shoot and asks Clearwing Audio to handle the production — starting a five-year relationship.
  • 1990 Clearwing Audio
    The World Wrestling Federation asks Clearwing Audio to provide the public address system, portable studios for backstage, and road cases for its WrestleMania event in Toronto — the first of several WrestleManias for Clearwing.
  • 1993 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio, a Milwaukee-based sound company, creates an agreement with Sunbelt Scenic Studios, a Phoenix-based lighting company, to trade equipment during their off-seasons.
  • 1995 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio becomes the production company for Summerfest, an annual music festival held along the lakefront in Milwaukee. Today, Summerfest is one of the biggest music events in the country, bringing bands and fans from across the country.
  • 1996 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio provides gear for the Red Hot Chili Peppers and Violent Femmes at Resolute Bay, Arctic Circle, North Pole, for the Molson Ice Polar Beach Party.
  • 1997 Clearwing Audio
    Miller Genuine Draft taps Clearwing Audio to provide audio and sound for its Blind Dates Tour.
  • 1998 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio purchases Creative Sound and Lighting.
  • 1999 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio handles the production for Natalie Cole’s tour.
  • 2001 Clearwing Audio
    The NFL hires Clearwing Audio for its Super Bowl pre-game tailgate party. Clearwing also now handles the Game Day Fan Plaza and sets up the NFL Experience. For the 2014 Super Bowl, Clearwing provided the concert stage for the Super Bowl Boulevard in Manhattan.
  • 2004 Clearwing Audio
    Clearwing Audio handles the production for the Steve Miller Band.
  • 2005 Clearwing Productions
    After Sunbelt Scenic Studios dissolves, Clearwing Audio sees the opportunity to do both sound and lighting in Milwaukee and in the Phoenix area and becomes Clearwing Productions with offices in Milwaukee, Wisconsin and Phoenix, Arizona.

    Clearwing Productions opens its Phoenix, Arizona Pro Shop to complement its Milwaukee, Wisconsin Pro Shop. Both offices are full-production houses with Production, Sales and Service departments.
  • 2007 Clearwing Productions
    Clearwing Productions handles the Jamestown 400th Anniversary Celebration.
  • 2009 Clearwing Productions
    Clearwing Productions, Arizona moves to its current location in Phoenix at 5640 S. 40th St.
  • 2011 Clearwing Productions
    The Foo Fighters hire Clearwing Productions for its tour.
  • 2013 Clearwing Productions
    Clearwing Productions completes an expansion of its Arizona office and warehouse and a remodel of its Pro Shop. The warehouse grew by 6,600 square feet and the office expanded by 1,400 square feet.
